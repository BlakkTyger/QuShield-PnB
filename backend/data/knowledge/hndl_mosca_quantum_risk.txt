HARVEST NOW DECRYPT LATER (HNDL) THREAT

Definition:
HNDL is an attack strategy where adversaries capture encrypted network traffic TODAY, store it, and decrypt it LATER once a Cryptographically Relevant Quantum Computer (CRQC) becomes available. The threat is active right now — passive collection requires no capabilities beyond what nation-state adversaries already possess.

Why It Matters for Banking:
Financial institutions transmit data with very long sensitivity lifetimes:
- Customer PII and KYC data: 7-10 year retention requirements (RBI KYC guidelines)
- Loan and mortgage agreements: 30-year sensitivity
- Audit trails and regulatory records: 10-year mandatory retention
- Trade secrets, mergers & acquisitions data: Indefinite sensitivity
- Authentication credentials (if key material is captured): Until credential rotation

HNDL Risk Calculation:
If data transmitted today could still be sensitive in 10 years, AND if a CRQC might arrive within 10 years, then:
- The data is AT RISK right now
- RSA and ECDH encryption protecting this data today is a PRESENT vulnerability
- Quantum-vulnerable key exchange must be replaced with ML-KEM IMMEDIATELY

Known HNDL Actors:
- Nation-state intelligence services are known to operate mass passive collection programs (PRISM, XKeyScore programs suggest capability)
- Chinese intelligence agencies have been specifically cited by CISA as conducting "harvest now decrypt later" operations against US financial infrastructure
- Attribution to other nation-states is harder but the capability is within reach of many

WHICH TRAFFIC IS AT RISK:
- HIGH: Any TLS session using RSA or ECDH key exchange protecting financial data
- HIGH: VPN tunnels using IKE with classical Diffie-Hellman
- MEDIUM: HTTPS sessions for customer authentication (credentials can be rotated, but session data may be sensitive)
- LOW: HMAC-authenticated sessions (symmetric MAC — not affected by HNDL/Shor's algorithm)
- NONE: AES-256 encrypted data at rest (quantum-safe symmetric encryption)

MOSCA'S INEQUALITY

Definition:
Mosca's Theorem states that if:
  X + Y > Z
then there is a security risk requiring immediate action, where:
  X = Time needed to migrate systems to quantum-safe cryptography (migration complexity)
  Y = Time the data/key material needs to remain secure (data sensitivity lifetime)
  Z = Time before a CRQC (Cryptographically Relevant Quantum Computer) arrives (threat timeline)

If X + Y > Z: ACT NOW — data is at risk
If X + Y = Z: ACT SOON — migration must start immediately to complete before CRQC
If X + Y < Z: PLAN CAREFULLY — migration can follow a structured timeline

CRQC Arrival Estimates:
Expert consensus ranges widely, but representative estimates:
- Optimistic (earliest possible): 2028-2030
- Median estimate (most likely): 2033-2035
- Pessimistic (delayed breakthrough): 2040-2050
- The uncertainty itself is a risk factor — planning for the median case (2033) is the standard approach

Migration Time Estimates by System Type:
- External TLS (web servers, load balancers): 6-18 months
- PKI and Certificate infrastructure: 12-24 months
- Core banking system integration: 24-48 months
- HSM and key management migration: 18-36 months
- Legacy/mainframe systems: 36-60 months

MOSCA ANALYSIS EXAMPLES FOR BANKING:

Example 1: Internet-facing API Gateway (RSA key exchange)
X (migration time) = 12 months
Y (data sensitivity) = 10 years (customer financial data with 10-year retention)
Z (CRQC median estimate) = 2033 = ~9 years from 2024
Result: 1 + 10 = 11 > 9 → URGENT. Start migration NOW.

Example 2: Internal management portal (ECDH key exchange)
X = 18 months
Y = 2 years (operational data, short retention)
Z = 9 years
Result: 1.5 + 2 = 3.5 < 9 → LOW RISK. Plan migration within 3-4 years.

Example 3: Core banking replication (RSA-4096 key exchange)
X = 48 months (complex legacy migration)
Y = 30 years (mortgage data, long retention)
Z = 9 years
Result: 4 + 30 = 34 >> 9 → CRITICAL. Even if migration started immediately, data remains at risk for decades. This is the most critical class of risk.

RISK CLASSIFICATION BASED ON MOSCA INEQUALITY:
- QUANTUM CRITICAL (Score 800-1000): X + Y > Z + 5 years. Data already at significant risk. Immediate migration required.
- QUANTUM VULNERABLE (Score 600-800): X + Y > Z. Migration must begin within 6 months.
- AT RISK (Score 400-600): X + Y ≈ Z. Migration must begin within 12-18 months.
- QUANTUM AWARE (Score 200-400): X + Y < Z by 2-3 years. Migration planned, monitoring required.
- QUANTUM READY (Score 0-200): PQC algorithms deployed, or data is not quantum-vulnerable.

QUANTUM RISK SCORE CALCULATION (QuShield Methodology):
Base score derived from:
1. Algorithm vulnerability (RSA/ECDH: max 300 points; ECC: 250 points; classical DH: 300 points)
2. HNDL exposure (externally accessible endpoint: +200 points)
3. Data sensitivity (financial PII, >5 year retention: +200 points)
4. Key length adequacy (key < 2048 bits: +100 points)
5. TLS version (TLS 1.0/1.1 active: +100 points; no forward secrecy: +50 points)
6. Migration complexity (legacy system, no vendor PQC roadmap: +100 points)
Maximum score: 1000 (all factors at maximum risk)
