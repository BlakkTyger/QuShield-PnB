RBI IT Framework and Post-Quantum Cryptography Requirements for Indian Banks
Source: Reserve Bank of India IT Framework for Banks 2023, RBI Circular RBI/2023-24/85, DPSS.CO.OD.No.S-1143/06.08.005/2023-24
Tags: rbi, compliance, india, banking, regulatory, pqc, crypto_governance

REGULATORY CONTEXT
The Reserve Bank of India (RBI) issued updated IT security guidelines through its IT Framework for Banks (2023) and subsequent circulars mandating that Scheduled Commercial Banks (SCBs), Urban Cooperative Banks (UCBs), and payment system operators assess and plan for post-quantum cryptographic transitions. These guidelines align with NIST's post-quantum standardization and anticipate CRQC (Cryptographically Relevant Quantum Computer) threats to existing PKI infrastructure.

KEY REGULATORY REQUIREMENTS

1. CRYPTOGRAPHIC INVENTORY (CBOM)
Banks must maintain a Cryptographic Bill of Materials (CBOM) for all systems handling financial data. The CBOM must identify:
- All asymmetric cryptographic algorithms in use (RSA, ECDSA, ECDH, DH)
- Key lengths and expiry dates
- Certificate authorities and chain of trust
- Cryptographic dependencies in third-party software
- Annual CBOM audit submission to the CISO/Board Risk Committee

2. QUANTUM RISK ASSESSMENT
Each bank must perform a formal Quantum Risk Assessment covering:
- "Harvest Now, Decrypt Later" (HNDL) threat exposure: sensitive data encrypted with RSA/ECC that could be harvested today and decrypted by future quantum computers
- Mosca's Inequality analysis: if (migration time + data sensitivity lifetime) > expected CRQC arrival, migration is urgent
- Classification of assets by quantum risk: Critical (immediate action), High (18-month plan), Medium (36-month plan)

3. CRYPTOGRAPHIC AGILITY MANDATE
Banks must implement and demonstrate cryptographic agility — the ability to rapidly swap cryptographic algorithms without system redesign. Requirements:
- Abstract cryptographic operations behind configurable interfaces
- No hard-coded algorithm choices in production code
- Maintain algorithm configuration in HSMs or secure key management systems
- Documented rollback procedures for algorithm changes

4. TLS AND NETWORK SECURITY
- TLS 1.3 is mandatory for all external-facing services as of January 2024 (RBI Circular 2023)
- TLS 1.0 and 1.1 must be disabled
- SSL 3.0 must be disabled
- Cipher suites must include forward secrecy (ECDHE or DHE key exchange)
- Banks must plan hybrid PQC TLS (X25519MLKEM768) deployment by 2026 for internet-facing services
- All inter-bank API communication (NeSL, NACH, UPI) must use TLS 1.3 with planned PQC migration

5. CERTIFICATE MANAGEMENT
- Certificate validity periods must not exceed 2 years (RBI guideline aligned with CA/Browser Forum)
- Certificates issued after 2025 should be from CAs with documented PQC migration roadmaps
- Banks must maintain certificate transparency (CT) logging for all public-facing certificates
- Wildcard certificates restricted: critical banking services must use individual certificates

6. THIRD-PARTY AND VENDOR RISK
- Technology vendors, cloud providers, and fintech partners must provide PQC readiness assessments
- Vendor contracts must include cryptographic agility SLAs
- Core banking system (CBS) vendors must commit to PQC-ready software versions by 2027
- Payment gateway integrations must support TLS 1.3 with PQC transition plan

7. REGULATORY DEADLINES
- Q1 2024: Complete CBOM inventory submission
- Q3 2024: Quantum risk assessment completed and submitted to Board Risk Committee
- Q1 2025: Cryptographic agility framework implemented
- Q4 2025: TLS 1.3 enforcement for all external services
- Q2 2026: Pilot hybrid PQC deployment on non-critical systems
- Q1 2027: PQC deployment on all critical payment and settlement systems
- Q4 2028: Complete phase-out of RSA/ECC from internet-facing production systems

8. REPORTING AND GOVERNANCE
- CISO must report quantum risk posture to Board Risk Committee quarterly
- Annual independent assessment of PQC readiness by CERT-In empanelled auditor
- Incident reporting: any cryptographic weakness discovered in production must be reported to RBI within 6 hours

PENALTIES FOR NON-COMPLIANCE
RBI has authority to impose financial penalties under Section 47A(1)(c) of the Banking Regulation Act 1949. Security lapses including inadequate cryptographic controls may result in:
- Monetary penalties (up to ₹1 crore per violation)
- Operational restrictions on digital services
- Mandatory third-party audit requirements
- Public disclosure of compliance failures

NPCI-SPECIFIC REQUIREMENTS
- UPI transaction signing must use approved algorithms (currently ECDSA-P256; PQC migration plan required by 2026)
- IMPS and NEFT message authentication codes (MACs) must migrate to HMAC-SHA3 or PQC equivalents
- NACH mandates must verify bank signatures — PQC compatibility required in clearing infrastructure
